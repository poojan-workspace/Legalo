Does the buyer have a statutory right to cancel the transaction?

What is the statutory right to cancel?

Federal and state laws allow buyers a 3-day "cooling off" period for certain sales, namely sales made and solicited at the buyer's home. If the transaction falls under one of these state or federal laws, the seller must disclose to the buyer in the contract of his or her right to cancel the transaction. In addition, the seller must provide 2 copies of a cancellation form.

What are the different types of dispute resolution?

Court litigation is when a party files an action or claim in court and each side presents its case or defense in a trial for a judge or jury to determine a final outcome on the claim. The decision by the judge or jury is final and binding.

Arbitration is when an arbitrator, a neutral third party selected by the parties, evaluates the dispute and determines a settlement. The decision by the arbitrator is final and binding.

Mediation is when a mediator, a neutral third party selected by the parties, tries to facilitate a compromise and agreement. The decision by the mediator is nonbinding.
